## ðŸš€ Setting Up in the App

### Step 1: Create New Draft
1. Open the app
2. Go to Dashboard
3. Click "Create New Draft" or use the draft manager
4. Name it: "Boston Chops DTX - [Your New Concept Name]"

### Step 2: Set Restaurant Type
1. Go to **Financial Projections** tab
2. Scroll to "Restaurant Type & Initial Costs"
3. Select **"Existing Restaurant"**
4. Enter the **Purchase Price** from the PDF

### Step 3: Configure Financial Data

#### A. Startup Costs (Existing Restaurant)
- **Purchase Price**: Enter from PDF
- **Include Renovations?**: Check if you need renovations
- **Renovation Costs**: Enter your renovation budget

#### B. Rent Calculator
1. Scroll to "Planning Calculators" â†’ "Rent Calculator"
2. Enter:
   - Square Footage
   - Rent per sq ft (or total annual rent)
   - Lease Term
   - Annual Increase %
   - CAM charges (if applicable)
3. Click "Apply to Financial Projections"

#### C. Sales Projections
1. Use "Sales Projections Calculator"
2. If you have current revenue data, use it as the base
3. Adjust for your new concept
4. Select scenario (Conservative/Moderate/Optimistic)
5. Click "Apply to Financial Projections"

### Step 4: Business Plan Sections

#### Executive Summary
1. Go to **Executive Summary** tab
2. Enter:
   - Restaurant Name (your new name)
   - Business Type (your new concept)
   - Location: Downtown Crossing / Financial District
   - Use AI Assistant to help write sections

#### Market Analysis
1. Go to **Market Analysis** tab
2. Use AI Research Panel to get market insights for Downtown Crossing
3. Fill in:
   - Target Market
   - Competitive Analysis
   - Market Trends

### Step 5: Use AI to Help

#### AI Financial Advisor
- Ask: "What should I consider when buying an existing restaurant?"
- Ask: "What are the risks of changing a restaurant concept?"
- Ask: "How much should I budget for renovations?"

#### AI Writing Assistant

## ðŸ’¡ Tips for Existing Restaurant Purchase

1. **Due Diligence**: Verify all financials, lease terms, and equipment condition
2. **Concept Change**: Consider costs of rebranding, menu changes, and customer retention
3. **Renovations**: Factor in downtime and lost revenue during renovations
4. **Staff**: Determine if you'll keep existing staff or hire new
5. **Licenses**: Verify all permits and licenses transfer properly
6. **Inventory**: Negotiate inventory value at closing

## ðŸ“Š Key Metrics to Track

- **Total Investment**: Purchase Price + Renovations + Working Capital
- **Break-even Timeline**: When will you recover your investment?
