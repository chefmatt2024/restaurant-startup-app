## Overview
This roadmap guides you through the entire process of buying an existing restaurant, from finding the right opportunity to opening day. Use this alongside the app to track your progress.

**Estimated Timeline**: 3-6 months (depending on complexity)

---

## Phase 1: Discovery & Evaluation (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1-2: Find Opportunities
**Goal**: Identify potential restaurants for sale

#### Tasks:
- [ ] **Search Listings**
  - Check business broker websites (BizBuySell, LoopNet, BusinessBroker.net)
  - Contact commercial real estate agents specializing in restaurants
  - Network with restaurant industry contacts
  - Check local business associations
  - Look for "For Sale" signs in neighborhoods you're interested in

- [ ] **Create Search Criteria**
  - Location preferences (neighborhoods, foot traffic, parking)
  - Price range
  - Size (square footage, seating capacity)
  - Concept type (or convertible to your concept)
  - Lease terms (years remaining, renewal options)

- [ ] **Use App Tools**
  - Create draft project in app
  - Use Market Analysis to research neighborhoods
  - Use Rent Calculator to understand market rates
  - Set up financial projections template

#### Deliverables:
- List of 5-10 potential opportunities
- Neighborhood analysis for each location
- Initial financial feasibility assessment

---

### Week 3-4: Initial Evaluation
**Goal**: Narrow down to 2-3 serious candidates

#### Tasks:
- [ ] **Property Visits**
  - Visit during different times (lunch, dinner, off-peak)
  - Observe foot traffic and customer flow
  - Note condition of equipment, fixtures, and space
  - Check parking availability
  - Assess neighborhood vibe and competition

- [ ] **Initial Financial Review**
  - Request P&L statements (last 2-3 years)
  - Review tax returns
  - Check current rent and lease terms
  - Estimate renovation needs
  - Calculate total investment required

- [ ] **Use App Tools**
  - Enter purchase price in Financial Projections
  - Use Rent Calculator with actual lease terms
  - Create sales projections based on current revenue
  - Calculate break-even scenarios
  - Use AI Financial Advisor: "What should I look for when evaluating a restaurant purchase?"

#### Key Questions to Answer:
- Why is the restaurant for sale?
- What's the current revenue trend (growing, stable, declining)?
- What's included in the sale (equipment, inventory, licenses)?
- Are there any liens or outstanding debts?
- What's the condition of the lease?
- What permits/licenses transfer?

#### Deliverables:
- Evaluation checklist for each property
- Initial financial analysis
- Pros/cons comparison of top candidates

---

## Phase 2: Due Diligence (Weeks 5-8)

### Week 5-6: Deep Financial Analysis
**Goal**: Verify all financial claims and assess true value

#### Tasks:
- [ ] **Financial Documents Review**
  - [ ] 3 years of tax returns (business and personal if applicable)
  - [ ] 3 years of P&L statements
  - [ ] Bank statements (last 12 months)
  - [ ] Accounts receivable/payable
  - [ ] Inventory valuation
  - [ ] Equipment list with values
  - [ ] Outstanding debts and liabilities
  - [ ] Employee payroll records

- [ ] **Financial Analysis in App**
  - Enter actual revenue data into Sales Projections
  - Compare seller's numbers to your projections
  - Identify discrepancies and red flags
  - Calculate adjusted EBITDA
  - Assess working capital needs
  - Use AI: "Analyze these financials for a restaurant purchase"

- [ ] **Valuation**
  - Calculate fair market value
  - Compare to asking price
  - Assess if price is reasonable
  - Identify negotiation points

#### Red Flags to Watch For:
- Declining revenue trends
- High debt-to-revenue ratio
- Unusual expenses or write-offs
- Missing or incomplete records
- Discrepancies between tax returns and P&L

---

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Skipping Due Diligence**: Don't rush - verify everything
2. **Underestimating Renovation Costs**: Get multiple quotes
3. **Ignoring Lease Terms**: Understand all lease obligations
4. **Not Planning for Transition**: Have a detailed transition plan
5. **Underestimating Working Capital**: You need cash for first 3-6 months
6. **Changing Too Much Too Fast**: Gradual changes often work better
7. **Not Retaining Key Staff**: Some existing staff knowledge is valuable
8. **Poor Communication**: Keep employees, suppliers, and customers informed
